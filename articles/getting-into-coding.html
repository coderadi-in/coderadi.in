how i got into coding

Documented by coderadi
keyboard_arrow_down

TIMELINE

One of the weirdest thing about me is how I got into coding. I got into coding from a YouTube short, just one.

I started thinking I'll try for a week, but got addicted, a simple habit loop led me to code for 3 years.

Now I'm helping businesses through my skill.

chapter I

getting different from others

While I was in eighth standard I was pretty much same as others. However some things was really different, like I was always trying out new things. So when I was in the 8th standard, one evening I was scrolling YouTube shorts, I got a short in which a person was taking a challenge to learn coding in one day.

I was pretty different since I got in 6th standard. In 5th standard I became class topper, in 6th standard I tried out writing some encyclopedia about prehistoric creatures, science topics, and space, in 7th standard I was checking out building electric project.

After watching the short, I think I could also try it out, I'll try for a week and I'll get enough info to flex in friends. I searched for some tutorials on YouTube. And thankfully I got one the best python playlist, the mentor had a great teaching skills.

With his friendly talking behavior, the first two videos was not as clear, but the third video was amazing, I tried out some code, it worked I feel happy, I coded more, I feel more happy. In this loop, I completed the full playlist in a month.

Then I learnt HTML, CSS, JS, then tried out some other frameworks like the Flask, NodeJS, React, and much more.

chapter II

MEANWHILE I THOUGHT TO TRY OUT READING SOME BOOKS

I tried out THINK AND GROW RICH by Napoleon Hill. After reading, I bought one more, and again, it became a habit, currently I've read more than 15 books.

Now it's an addiction, I'm addicted to coding and reading. But I was never supposed to build a business around my skills, it was some cars and a dream of free life, world-tour, and missing things in my life, that led me build a business.

And now I think I'm pretty different from other people in my locality and my country of my age. And while building all these stuff, I lost one thing, I don't like school anymore, I got 73.5% in 10th, known as below-average student in 11th, and I think I'm not gonna complete my 12th.
